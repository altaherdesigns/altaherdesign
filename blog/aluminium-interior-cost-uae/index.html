Aluminium Interior Cost in the UAE:
Price Per Square Foot (2026)

Aluminium has quietly become one of the most popular materials for UAE interiors — kitchens, wardrobes, cladding and partitions — because it shrugs off humidity, heat and termites. But what does it actually cost? Here are honest 2026 per-square-foot ranges and what moves the number.

"Aluminium interior" covers a lot of ground — kitchen cabinetry, wardrobe carcasses, decorative wall cladding, ceiling features and room partitions can all be built in aluminium. That breadth is exactly why a single price-per-square-foot figure can be misleading: a flat cladding panel and a fully-fitted aluminium kitchen are priced on completely different bases. So this guide breaks the cost down by application, gives indicative ranges, and explains what decides where your project lands.

We fabricate aluminium interiors in-house in Fujairah and install across all seven emirates, so these figures reflect real UAE market conditions in 2026 — not generic numbers.

Why aluminium for interiors at all?

Before cost, it helps to understand why aluminium is specified so often in UAE homes. Timber and MDF can struggle in Gulf conditions — humidity and the occasional leak cause swelling, warping and delamination over the years, and termites are a real risk. Aluminium doesn't absorb moisture, doesn't warp, won't feed termites, is non-combustible, and takes powder-coat colours and convincing woodgrain finishes that last. For kitchens, bathrooms, wardrobes and any humidity-exposed area, that durability is the whole argument.

What drives the per-square-foot figure

Within those ranges, five things decide your number:

  • System and profile — a simple sheet-clad panel costs far less than an engineered profile system with concealed fixings.
  • Thickness and grade — heavier-gauge aluminium and marine-grade alloys cost more but last longer and feel more solid.
  • Finish — standard powder-coat colours are cheapest; premium metallics, anodised finishes and realistic woodgrain sublimation cost more.
  • Complexity — curves, 3D patterns, integrated lighting and tight tolerances add fabrication time.
  • Site and access — height, the number of cut-outs and how easy the space is to work in all affect installation cost.
"The honest way to read 'cost per square foot' for interiors: treat it as a budgeting starting point, not a quote. Two aluminium kitchens of the same size can differ by 60% on finish and hardware alone."

Aluminium vs the alternatives, on cost

For cladding and partitions, aluminium is genuinely cost-effective — competitive upfront and very low-maintenance over its life. For cabinetry, quality aluminium typically sits around or a little above mid-range timber/MDF on day one, but the lifetime maths often favours it in the UAE: it won't swell from a leak under the sink, won't warp in a humid kitchen, and won't need replacing as soon. If your priority is the lowest possible upfront cost and the area stays dry, laminate MDF still wins on price; if you want longevity in a kitchen, bathroom or anywhere humid, aluminium earns its premium.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does an aluminium interior cost per square foot in the UAE?

As a 2026 guide, roughly AED 40–120 per square foot for wall cladding and panelling, AED 50–150 for partitions, and more for cabinetry (usually quoted per linear metre). The system, finish, thickness and installation all move the figure, so we confirm with a written quote.

Is aluminium interior better value than wood?

For dry decorative areas at the lowest upfront cost, laminate MDF is cheaper. For kitchens, bathrooms, wardrobes and humid areas, aluminium usually wins on lifetime value because it won't warp, swell or attract termites in UAE conditions.

Do you fabricate and install across the UAE?

Yes. We fabricate aluminium interiors in our Fujairah workshop and install across all seven emirates. Delivery and installation are included in the quote based on your location.
